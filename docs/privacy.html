CipherSheet Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 13, 2026

This Privacy Policy applies to the CipherSheet Google Sheets add-on and the CipherSheet website. It explains how CipherSheet accesses, uses, stores, and shares Google user data.

Who We Are

CipherSheet is an independent open-source project. Project homepage: https://kwikwag.github.io/CipherSheet/.

Google User Data We Access

CipherSheet uses the minimum Google Workspace scopes needed for its features.

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/spreadsheets.currentonly: Access to the current spreadsheet to read and write selected cell values, apply/remove warning protections, and write/remove cell notes used by CipherSheet.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/script.container.ui: Needed to show the CipherSheet sidebar and dialogs in Google Sheets.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/userinfo.email: Used only when optional audit logging is enabled, to record the acting user email in the hidden audit log sheet.

How We Use Google User Data

  • Encrypt plaintext entered by the user and write ciphertext into the selected sheet cell.
  • Decrypt ciphertext locally in the browser when a valid key is loaded.
  • If you choose Reveal, send plaintext to Apps Script only to write it back to the selected cell.
  • Optionally maintain an in-spreadsheet audit log when enabled by the sheet owner.
  • Store add-on settings (for example, audit log enabled/disabled) in Google Apps Script document properties.

What We Do Not Do With Google User Data

  • We do not sell Google user data.
  • We do not use Google user data for advertising (including targeted, interest-based, or retargeted ads).
  • We do not transfer Google user data to data brokers, information resellers, or for credit or lending purposes.
  • We do not use Google user data to train generalized AI/ML models.

Sharing and Disclosure

CipherSheet does not run a backend that receives your sheet data. Data is processed in your browser and Google Apps Script within your Google account context.

  • No sale or unrelated sharing: we do not disclose Google user data to third parties for unrelated purposes.
  • Google infrastructure: data needed for add-on functionality is handled through Google Sheets and Google Apps Script services.
  • Collaborators: spreadsheet content (including encrypted values, notes, and optional audit log rows) may be visible to people you share the spreadsheet with according to your Google Sheets sharing settings.

Storage, Retention, and Deletion

  • No CipherSheet servers: CipherSheet does not store spreadsheet content, encryption keys, or Google user data on CipherSheet-owned servers.
  • Encryption keys: keys are generated/downloaded locally and remain under your control.
  • Spreadsheet content: ciphertext/plaintext stored in your sheet remains until you or collaborators edit/delete it.
  • Document settings: stored in Apps Script document properties and retained until changed or removed.
  • Temporary cache: short-lived consent/heartbeat cache entries are retained for seconds (for example, approximately 4-60 seconds) and then expire automatically.
  • Audit logs: if enabled, audit rows are retained in the hidden CipherSheet_AuditLog sheet until you disable logging and/or delete that sheet/workbook.

You can delete your data by clearing relevant cells, deleting audit log sheets, removing document properties, uninstalling the add-on, or deleting the spreadsheet file in Google Drive.

Security Protections

  • Client-side encryption/decryption uses the browser Web Crypto API and AES-256-GCM.
  • The key file is not uploaded by CipherSheet and is not recoverable by CipherSheet if lost.
  • Data access follows least privilege scopes required for user-facing functionality.

Policy Changes

We may update this policy when functionality or data handling changes. We will revise the "Last updated" date and publish the updated policy at this URL.
